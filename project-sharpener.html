Project overview

This project involved redesigning the Staples Desk Mate consumer electric pencil sharpener using Design for Manufacturing and Assembly (DFMA) principles to improve manufacturability, assembly efficiency, cost, and safety.

Industry-standard DFMA software was used to perform Boothroyd-Dewhurst DFA and Lucas Method analysis, guiding part reduction and assembly simplification. Multiple redesign concepts were compared using a weighted decision matrix, and a Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) was conducted to ensure safety, reliability, and functional performance.

Design process
  • Analysed the original electric pencil sharpener to understand system function, subassemblies, and assembly sequence
  • Identified key issues: high part count, fastener-heavy construction, using complexity, and packaging inefficiencies
  • Used industry-standard DFMA software to evaluate the baseline design and applied Boothroyd-Dewhurst analysis to assess assembly, drive, handling, and insertion difficulty
  • Applied Lucas Method to classify assembly elements as non-essential components and evaluate design efficiency
  • Developed multiple redesign concepts focused on reducing part count, simplifying assembly, and improving manufacturability
  • Compared concepts using a weighted decision matrix based on performance, manufacturability, cost, and sustainability
  • Conducted FMEA to identify and mitigate safety, reliability, and durability risks
  • Incorporated risk-mitigation strategies into the final design

Results

The redesigned sharpener reduced part count by eliminating redundant and non-value-adding components identified through DFMA analysis, simplified the assembly process by reducing fastener usage and improving overall assembly flow, and decreased assembly time and handling complexity using insights from DFMA software evaluations.

  • Reduced part count by eliminating redundant components identified through DFMA
  • Simplified assembly process by reducing fastener usage and improving flow
  • Decreased assembly time and handling complexity
  • Improved manufacturability and design efficiency while maintaining product performance and user requirements
