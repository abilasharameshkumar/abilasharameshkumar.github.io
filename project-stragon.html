Project Overview

My team and I designed the "Stragon," an innovative wagon-stroller hybrid that addressed the limitations of traditional strollers by emphasising safety, accessibility, and user comfort.

By integrating the strongest features from both a wagon chassis and a stroller - including retractable seats, an adjustable handlebar, and a hybrid suspension system - we developed a solution that works better for a wider range of users.

Our design process reduced user exclusion rates from 15.5% to 11.8%, showing measurable improvements in inclusivity. I contributed significantly to CAD modelling, engineering drawings, and human factors calculations throughout.

Key Design Features
  • Dual terrain system (hand and foot) for safety on varied terrains
  • Removable, rotatable child seats with harness systems for flexibility and security
  • Mobility walker-style handlebar for improved ergonomics and accessibility
  • Shock-absorbing suspension and hybrid wheel system (track and caster) for stability
  • Integrated storage and accessibility ramp for user convenience
Design Process

The design process followed an iterative, human-centred approach. Three rounds of concept development were carried out, each focusing on different priority areas and validated using the Cambridge Exclusion Calculator (CEC).
