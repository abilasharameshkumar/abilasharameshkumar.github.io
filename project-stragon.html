Project overview

My team and I designed the "Stragon," an innovative wagon-stroller hybrid that addressed the limitations of traditional strollers by emphasising safety, accessibility, and user comfort.

By integrating the strongest features from both a wagon chassis, retractable seats, and an adjustable handlebar, we developed a series of design iterations before finalising a solution. Our design process reduced user exclusion rates from 15.5% to 11.8%, showing measurable improvements in inclusivity.

Throughout the project, I contributed significantly to CAD modelling, engineering drawings, and human factors calculations, ensuring manufacturability and inclusive design.

Key design features
  • Dual terrain system (hand + foot) for safety on varied terrains
  • Removable, rotatable child seats with harness systems for flexibility and security
  • Mobility walker-style handlebar for improved ergonomics and accessibility
  • Shock-absorbing suspension and hybrid wheel system (track + caster) for stability and smooth rides
  • Integrated storage and accessibility ramp for user convenience
Design process

The design process followed an iterative, human-centred approach using the Cambridge Exclusion Calculator (CEC) analysis and human factors testing. Three rounds of concept development were carried out, each focusing on different priorities.
