Project overview

Designed, manufactured, and tested a tractor-pull slot car capable of climbing a 30° inclined track under increasing loads (10g, 20g, and 50g). The project emphasised design for manufacturing, traction optimisation, and iterative testing under real constraints.

The final manufactured slot car was raced against other teams, earning second place — losing to first by only 0.5 seconds.

Design process

The design process followed a structured approach emphasising manufacturability, traction, and weight distribution.

  • Developed multiple design iterations using a morphological chart
  • Evaluated gear-driven vs. direct-drive systems — experimental testing showed direct-drive superior performance
  • Prioritised traction and weight distribution over mechanical gear reduction
  • Modelled system as a block on an inclined plane to account for frictional forces, weight distribution, and traction limits
  • Compared theoretical predictions with experimental climb times

  • Wheels, pulleys, motor fixtures

Results

The final tractor-pull slot car successfully met all performance requirements, completing all three competition heats (10g, 20g, 50g) and climbing the full 30° inclined track under maximum load. Awarded second place — losing to first by 0.5 seconds.
