Project overview

The objective of this capstone project was to analyse the Yardworks 4-Wheel Rolling All-Steel Garden Hose Reel Cart (250-ft capacity) and develop design improvements to enhance ergonomics, usability, and overall performance.

The project was motivated by the shortcomings of the existing design — poor usability and the significant effort required for manual rewinding and watering. These insights drove Victoria Street Engineering to develop a more efficient, user-friendly solution.

The redesigned caddy addresses key ergonomic and usability limitations by eliminating manual cranking and reducing user strain through the integration of a gas spring–assisted height adjustment and automated retraction system.

Key subsystems

Automation subassembly

The automation subassembly eliminates the need for manual cranking. A constant-force spring retraction mechanism enables automatic hose rewind, significantly reducing user effort during storage and operation.

Height adjustment mechanism

A gas spring-assisted telescoping height adjustment makes the caddy accessible to users of all heights. A ratchet-and-pawl mechanism allows locking at any length, with controlled release for smooth adjustment.

Materials & analysis

Material selection was guided by the need for strength, weather resistance, and manufacturability.

6061-T6
Aluminium for frame — high strength-to-weight ratio and excellent machinability
HDPE
Housing material — lightweight, weather-resistant, reduces overall caddy weight
S.S.
Stainless steel gas spring — corrosion-resistant and rated for repeated operation
4-wheel
All-terrain wide tires — tip-proof and stable across multiple terrain types
Results & conclusion

The redesigned hose caddy successfully addresses key ergonomic and usability limitations of existing models by eliminating manual cranking and reducing user strain. The integration of a gas spring–assisted height adjustment and automated retraction system results in a more efficient and user-friendly design.

The final solution provides a balanced approach to performance, manufacturability, and user needs. Load and structural calculations confirmed the design meets all identified requirements under real operating conditions.
