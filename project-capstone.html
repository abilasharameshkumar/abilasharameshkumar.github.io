Automated Garden Hose Caddy

A full redesign of an existing garden hose caddy into a market-ready product - engineered to eliminate manual cranking, improve ergonomics, and work for users of all heights and abilities.

Project Overview

The objective of this capstone project was to take an existing consumer garden hose caddy and redesign it into a practical, market-ready product that addresses its key usability shortcomings.

The original Yardworks design required significant manual effort to rewind and use, had limited height adjustability, and didn't work well across different terrains. Our team, Victoria Street Engineering, set out to solve all of that in a single cohesive redesign.

The result is a caddy with automated hose retraction, a gas spring-assisted telescoping height system, and an all-terrain wheel configuration - making it accessible and easy to use for a wide range of people.

Key Subsystems

Automation Subassembly

The automation subassembly eliminates the need for manual cranking. A constant-force spring retraction mechanism enables automatic hose rewind, significantly reducing user effort during storage and operation.

Height Adjustment Mechanism

A gas spring-assisted telescoping system makes the caddy accessible to users of all heights. A ratchet-and-pawl mechanism allows locking at any length with controlled release for smooth adjustment.

Results and Conclusion

The redesigned caddy successfully addresses the ergonomic and usability limitations of existing models. By eliminating manual cranking and integrating height adjustment, the product is meaningfully more accessible and easier to use for a wide range of people.

Load and structural calculations confirmed the design meets all requirements under real operating conditions. The final solution balances performance, manufacturability, and user needs.

Future Work
  • Greater emphasis on material selection to further reduce overall weight
  • Simplify redundant structural members in the base frame
  • Add storage compartments once weight targets are met
  • Earlier start to detailed design for better team coordination
