Project overview

For this project, I designed a vibration absorber system aimed at mitigating rotor-induced vibrations in the tail section of a helicopter. The goal was to address a rotor imbalance in the tail section of a helicopter, where a 100g unbalanced mass created excessive vibrations. Left unresolved, these vibrations could compromise stability, structural life, and passenger safety.

To solve this, I designed a vibration absorber system tuned specifically for the rotor's operating range (1250–1750 rpm) using a single-degree-of-freedom (SDOF) spring-mass-damper model.

Key highlights
  • Modelled the absorber system as a single-degree-of-freedom (SDOF) spring-mass-damper
  • Conducted force and torque analysis to size the absorber for rotor-induced oscillation
  • Optimised absorber mass at 1.5 kg with spring stiffness of ~58,225 N/m
  • Modelled MATLAB frequency response and bandwidth plots showing effective mitigation within the operational range
  • Confirmed resonance control and stability improvements across rotor speeds
  • Created a SolidWorks model of the mass-spring absorber for integration into the tail rotor structure
